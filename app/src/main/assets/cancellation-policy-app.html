Apeerance Contract Cancellation Policy

Updated Aug 16, 2017

All refunds are of course subject to any processing fees.

Unsuccessful contract completion of appearance prior to start of services

Canceling More than 24 Hours Before Apeerance

If the peer initiates the cancellation, 100% refund to client will be provided, peer obviously gets nothing, and the client’s request will go back to the application to seek out a new peer to cover the matter.

If matter cannot be re-placed, then 100% is refunded back to the client.

Canceling Less than 24 Hours But More than 12 Hours Before Schedule Apeerance

If peer initiates the cancellation, peer will received no payment. Peer will be banned from Apeerance indefinitely unless there is some legitimate, valid excuse for the failure to appear.

Client’s request is placed back in the pool of requests and Apeerance attempts to re-place the case. If the case cannot be re-placed, then 100% of the fee is refunded to client.

If client initiates the cancellation, 50% refund will be provided. If the case was placed with a peer attorney, Peer will receive 25% of the fee to be earned. If the case was not placed yet, the client will get 100% refund.

Canceling Less than 12 Hours Before

If peer initiates the cancellation, peer will received no payment. Peer will be banned from Apeerance indefinitely unless there is some legitimate, valid excuse for the failure to appear.

Client’s request is placed back in the pool of requests and Apeerance attempts to re-place the case. If the case cannot be re-placed then 100% of the fee is refunded to client.

If client initiates the cancellation, no refund will be provided. Peer will receive 50% of the fee to be earned. If case is not placed, the client will get 50% refund.

Post completion/dissatisfied with the service refund procedure

If you as the client are unsatisfied with the service you received, then during the review cycle, when 1 or 2 stars are given, you will be prompted to request refund. At that point, the system will engage in an email exchange between the client and the peer to resolve the issue.

If a peer has completed the service, i.e., an appearance was made, then no guarantee of a refund can be promised. As with all risks inherent to service transactions, there is a risk of poor performance that cannot be guaranteed. Malpractice is covered by our malpractice policy.
